THE DIVISION OF HUMANKIND: HYPNOS AND CLEERS

HYPNOS – The Hypnotizable (approximately 65–75% of the population)

Origin: The term derives from the Latin «hypnos» (sleep) and the suffix «-oid» (form, kind, type).

Definition: Hypnos are individuals who are susceptible to various forms of suggestive influence, including mass communication, social cues, and groupthink. They are aligned with the collective mental stream — also referred to as the hypotron — which amplifies and standardizes emotional and cognitive responses within a population.

CLEERS – The Non-Hypnotizable (approximately 25–35% of the population)

Definition: Cleers are individuals who demonstrate a higher degree of autonomy in cognition and emotional self-regulation. They are immune to mass suggestibility and do not absorb unfiltered messaging from collective inputs.

KEY DIFFERENTIATORS

Perception: Hypnos internalize external consensus; Cleers question and analyze it independently.

Emotional Reactivity: Hypnos exhibit high emotional entrainment with media and peer influence; Cleers maintain emotional sovereignty.

Decision-Making: Hypnos rely on group-validated truths; Cleers process reality based on direct perception and critical insight.
